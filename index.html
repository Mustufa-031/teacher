SECTION 06

Stellar Timeline

TIME // 4.6 BYR
4.6 BILLION YEARS AGO

Formation of the Solar System

A giant molecular cloud collapsed under its own gravity, beginning to spin and flatten into a protoplanetary disk. Most of the mass gathered at the center to form the Sun.

4.5 BILLION YEARS AGO

Theia Collision & Moon Birth

A Mars-sized body named Theia collided with the early Earth. The debris ejected into orbit eventually coalesced to form the Moon, stabilizing Earth's axial tilt.

4.0 – 3.5 BILLION YEARS AGO

The Great Bombardment & First Life

The inner planets endured a heavy asteroid bombardment. On Earth, in the harsh conditions of the early oceans, the first single-celled organisms emerged.

66 MILLION YEARS AGO

Cretaceous-Paleogene Extinction

A massive asteroid impact in the Yucatán Peninsula caused a global climate catastrophe, wiping out 75% of plant and animal species on Earth.

1957 CE

The Dawn of Spaceflight

Humanity launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, marking the beginning of the Space Age and our physical exploration of the cosmos.

1969 CE

Apollo 11 Lunar Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon, a watershed moment in human history that proved we were no longer bound to our home planet.

2021 CE

James Webb Space Telescope Launch

The most powerful space telescope ever constructed was launched, beginning a new era of infrared astronomy and peering further back in cosmic time than ever before.
