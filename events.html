Event Type // Orbital Alignment Earth-Moon-Sun

Solar Eclipse

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, thereby totally or partly obscuring the Sun's image for a viewer on Earth. This alignment can only happen during a new moon, when the Sun and Moon are in conjunction as seen from Earth.

Event Type // Shadow Projection Earth-Moon-Sun

Lunar Eclipse

A lunar eclipse occurs when the Moon moves into the Earth's shadow, causing the Moon to be darkened. This alignment can only occur during a full moon. Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse can be viewed from anywhere on the night side of Earth, often casting the Moon a haunting reddish hue.

The Big Bang

The leading explanation about how the universe began. At its simplest, it says the universe as we know it started with an infinitely hot, infinitely dense singularity, then inflated over the next 13.8 billion years to the vast, expanding cosmos we observe today. It is the event that started everything.

Event Type // Stellar Evolution (Future) ~5 Billion Years Ahead

Solar Giant Expansion

In approximately 5 billion years, the Sun will exhaust the hydrogen fuel in its core and begin to expand into a red giant. It will grow so massive that it will likely engulf the inner planets, including Earth. Eventually, it will shed its outer layers, leaving behind a glowing white dwarf core and a planetary nebula.
